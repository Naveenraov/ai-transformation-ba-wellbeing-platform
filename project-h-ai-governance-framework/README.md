### Responsible AI for a Clinical Environment

## What This Project Is

An internal responsible AI governance framework for Spectrum.Life — covering how AI is used, controlled, audited, and kept safe across the entire organisation.

Spectrum.Life already has world-class **external** AI governance. Cara is CE Class I certified, EU AI Act aligned, and validated in a randomised controlled trial. This framework brings that same standard **inside** — governing everything from a Case Manager's first Claude prompt to a fully autonomous Agentforce agent routing clinical contacts.

> **The governing principle:** AI at Spectrum.Life exists to serve people — the members, the staff, and the organisation. Every governance rule in this framework exists to protect that purpose and the people it serves.

---

## The Problem This Solves

Stephen is pushing 40-60% automation across the organisation. Claude is expanding to every department. Agentforce is being deployed for clinical triage routing. All of this without a formal internal governance structure creates real clinical, legal, and reputational risk in an environment handling special category health data for 9.8 million people.

This framework gives Stephen the confidence to scale, Lynda the structure to manage responsibly, and the board the ability to report accurately.

## The Four Risk Tiers

| Tier | Label | Examples | Key Governance Requirement |
|------|-------|----------|---------------------------|
| Tier 1 | Low Risk | Drafting internal emails, meeting summaries | Human review recommended |
| Tier 2 | Medium Risk | JD generation, client proposals, monthly reports | Human review required before action |
| Tier 3 | High Risk | Case notes, onboarding comms, EAP reports | Formal approval + GDPR Article 9 if health data |
| Tier 4 | Critical Risk | Clinical triage routing, any clinical care pathway | CCO sign-off + full audit trail + EU AI Act assessment |

---

## The Six Governance Principles

1. **Human Oversight** — A human is always responsible for every AI output that affects a person, a clinical decision, or a financial commitment
2. **Clinical Safety First** — In any conflict between efficiency and clinical safety, clinical safety wins. Always.
3. **Data Minimisation** — AI tools access only the data they need. No more.
4. **Transparency** — Staff and members know when AI is involved
5. **Accountability** — Every deployment has a named owner. No ambiguity.
6. **Continuous Improvement** — The framework is a living document, not a filed policy
